About

I'm a data science student at UNC Charlotte, working toward my B.S. with a background in Java, Python, C++, and Git — and a growing focus on the applied side: turning messy, half-formed business requests into working tools people actually use. During my last internship I built a time-tracking platform, a location explorer, an ordering system, and a reporting dashboard, each starting from unclear requirements and a stakeholder who wasn't quite sure what they wanted yet. That's usually where the interesting product decisions are.

I've also used KNIME to build and evaluate machine learning models (decision trees, logistic regression) on real employee attrition data, and I maintain my own fitness site end-to-end — design, front-end, and deployment — as a way to keep iterating on real, live software instead of only shipping once.
